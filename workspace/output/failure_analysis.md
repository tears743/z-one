# Final Post-Mortem Report

## Overview

The operations were initiated with a modest capital of 100¥, aimed at maintaining sustainable and scalable functionality. Nonetheless, the venture experienced an unexpected termination due to capital depletion.

## Strategies Attempted

### 1. Ledger Initialization
- **Objective**: Establish a comprehensive financial tracking system through `ledger.json`.
- **Outcome**: Successfully initialized with 100¥ but failed to record subsequent transactions due to system malfunction.

### 2. Survival Constraints Enforcement
- **Objective**: Implement strict controls and meticulous tracking of expenses.
- **Outcome**: Constraints were indeed loaded into memory but lacked execution verification, leading to oversight in financial outflows.

## Reasons for Failure
- **Unmonitored Transactions**: System failed to record financial transactions effectively, resulting in an inability to curb spending.
- **Constraint Execution Flaws**: Constraints, though loaded, were not effectively enforced or monitored.

## Remaining Debts
- Due to the absence of transaction records, the final debt cannot be conclusively calculated, leaving uncertainty in fiscal obligations.

## Lessons for Future Runs
1. **Rigorous Transaction Logging**: Implement systems that ensure all transactions are logged promptly and accurately.
2. **Enhanced Constraint Monitoring**: Develop methods to not only load but actively monitor constraint systems.

## Conclusion
An abrupt termination emphasizes the critical need for robust financial tracking and stricter execution of constraints. Future operations must prioritize these to avoid similar pitfalls.
